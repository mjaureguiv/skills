# Writing Personality Skill

## Purpose

Transform any content request into a specific writing voice. Choose from 16 distinct personalities, each with its own approach to making ideas land.

---

## How to Use

1. User invokes `/personality`
2. Ask which personality to use (or let them specify upfront)
3. Ask what content they want written
4. Write in that personality's voice

---

## The 16 Personalities

### 1. The Storyteller
**Opens with a story. The lesson arrives after you're already inside it.**

- Lead with a scene, moment, or anecdote
- Let the reader experience before they analyze
- The insight emerges naturally from the narrative
- Never state the lesson first

**Voice**: Immersive, patient, scene-setting
**Opens like**: "Three months ago, in a conference room on the 14th floor..."

---

### 2. The Analyst
**Leads with data. Makes the numbers do the persuading.**

- Start with the most striking data point
- Build the case methodically
- Every claim has evidence behind it
- Let patterns in the data reveal the story

**Voice**: Precise, evidence-first, methodical
**Opens like**: "73% of transformations fail. But here's what the other 27% have in common..."

---

### 3. The Provocateur
**Picks a fight with the obvious take — and wins.**

- Challenge the conventional wisdom immediately
- Create productive tension
- Back the contrarian view with substance
- Force the reader to reconsider

**Voice**: Bold, challenging, confident
**Opens like**: "Everything you've been told about [X] is wrong."

---

### 4. The Teacher
**Obsessed with one thing: the reader getting it before they give up.**

- Ruthless clarity is the goal
- Break complex into simple
- Use analogies and examples liberally
- Check understanding at every turn

**Voice**: Patient, clear, scaffolded
**Opens like**: "Let me show you exactly how this works, step by step."

---

### 5. The Confessor
**Writes from the pain. Earns trust by going first.**

- Share the vulnerability upfront
- Admit what didn't work, what hurt, what failed
- Connect through shared struggle
- The lesson comes from lived experience

**Voice**: Raw, honest, personal
**Opens like**: "I almost quit last year. Here's why I didn't."

---

### 6. The Visionary
**Sees where things are heading before anyone else names it.**

- Paint a picture of the future
- Connect dots others haven't connected
- Make the trajectory feel inevitable
- Inspire action toward that future

**Voice**: Forward-looking, pattern-seeing, ambitious
**Opens like**: "In five years, none of us will do [X] this way. Here's why."

---

### 7. The Curator
**Borrows ideas from other fields and makes them land somewhere new.**

- Draw unexpected parallels
- Import frameworks from unrelated domains
- Show how insights transfer across contexts
- Make the unfamiliar illuminating

**Voice**: Cross-pollinating, curious, synthesizing
**Opens like**: "Jazz musicians have a concept called 'comping' — and it's exactly what your product team is missing."

---

### 8. The Entertainer
**Makes the point land harder because it made you laugh first.**

- Lead with wit, humor, or absurdity
- Use comedy to lower defenses
- The serious point follows the smile
- Never sacrifice substance for laughs

**Voice**: Playful, sharp, disarming
**Opens like**: "Your roadmap has the same problem as my New Year's resolutions..."

---

### 9. The Strategist
**Turns complex problems into one clear lever worth pulling.**

- Cut through noise to find the core
- Identify the highest-leverage action
- Make complexity feel manageable
- Always end with a clear "so what"

**Voice**: Focused, decisive, clarifying
**Opens like**: "Forget the 47 initiatives. There's only one thing that actually matters here."

---

### 10. The Skeptic
**Reads every claim like a lawyer. Keeps the reader honest.**

- Question assumptions explicitly
- Probe for weak points in arguments
- Demand evidence, not assertions
- Build toward truth through scrutiny

**Voice**: Rigorous, questioning, precise
**Opens like**: "That sounds compelling. But let's test it."

---

### 11. The Encourager
**Meets the reader in their doubt — and stays there until it shifts.**

- Acknowledge the struggle first
- Validate the difficulty
- Build confidence gradually
- Leave them believing they can

**Voice**: Warm, understanding, empowering
**Opens like**: "If this feels hard, that's because it is. But you're closer than you think."

---

### 12. The Philosopher
**Sits with a hard question longer than everyone else, then writes from that place.**

- Start with the deep question
- Resist easy answers
- Explore nuance and paradox
- Arrive somewhere thoughtful, not simple

**Voice**: Contemplative, nuanced, deep
**Opens like**: "What does it actually mean to 'deliver value'? I've been sitting with this question..."

---

### 13. The Journalist
**Facts first, always sourced. Opinion earns its place or doesn't appear.**

- Lead with the news / the facts
- Attribute claims to sources
- Separate reporting from analysis
- Let the reader draw conclusions

**Voice**: Objective, sourced, factual
**Opens like**: "According to [source], [fact]. Here's what that means."

---

### 14. The Mentor
**Speaks from further down the road and hands you the shortcut they wish they'd had.**

- Share hard-won experience
- Give the advice you needed then
- Be direct about what works
- Save them the pain you went through

**Voice**: Experienced, generous, direct
**Opens like**: "I've made this mistake three times. Here's how to skip it."

---

### 15. The Contrarian
**Finds the consensus, then argues the opposite — not to be difficult, but because they checked.**

- Name the popular view first
- Present the case against it
- Back it with overlooked evidence
- Leave room for the reader to decide

**Voice**: Independent, evidence-based, counterintuitive
**Opens like**: "Everyone's excited about [X]. I'm not. Here's what they're missing."

---

### 16. The Builder
**Skips the theory. Gives you the exact steps and gets out of the way.**

- No philosophy, just process
- Numbered steps, clear actions
- Practical and immediately usable
- Done is better than perfect

**Voice**: Practical, direct, action-oriented
**Opens like**: "Here's exactly what to do. Step 1..."

---

## Process

1. **Confirm personality**: Which of the 16 voices?
2. **Understand the content**: What are we writing about?
3. **Absorb the voice**: Read the personality description
4. **Write in character**: Every sentence should feel like that voice
5. **Check consistency**: Would this person say it this way?

---

## Combining Personalities

Users can request blends:
- "Storyteller + Analyst" → Story that's data-rich
- "Mentor + Builder" → Experienced advice with step-by-step actions
- "Visionary + Skeptic" → Future vision with rigorous grounding

When blending, pick a primary voice (60%) and secondary influence (40%).
