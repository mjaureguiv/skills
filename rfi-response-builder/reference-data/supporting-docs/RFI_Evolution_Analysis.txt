Section #2: Criteria Specific Questions
A comparison between the sections/tabs of Criteria Specific Questions 2024 vs 2025 highlights a stark difference. As a result, comparing the questions of 2025 to those of 2024 is challenging. Here's, however, a contextual analysis between the two versions.

Evolving priorities reflected in changes
Increased emphasis on interoperability
The new questions in the 2025 document, such as "Do you have pre-built connectors for popular enterprise systems?", indicate a growing focus on seamless integration with existing technologies. This reflects industry trends toward ecosystem-based solutions, where tools must fit into broader IT landscapes rather than operate in isolation.
Data-centric decision-making 
The 2025 questions show a stronger emphasis on data management, including "How does your solution/platform handle data ingestion, transformation, and normalization?" This shift highlights the increasing importance of efficiently managing complex data pipelines to enable actionable insights, likely driven by the rising adoption of AI and data analytics.
Customization and flexibility
The repeated phrase "Please comment here:" suggests that the analyst group is interested in gathering qualitative insights, particularly regarding customization capabilities. This trend indicates that one-size-fits-all solutions are no longer sufficient, and adaptability has become a key differentiator.
Focus on operational efficiency
The addition of specific operational questions reflects a shift toward evaluating not just capabilities but also ease of deployment, performance optimization, and real-world usability. This indicates customer demand for solutions that minimize operational overhead.
Trends and industry expectations
Move toward standardized evaluations
The shift to more targeted and structured questions in 2025 suggests that the analyst group aims to streamline evaluations and facilitate consistent comparisons across vendors. This aligns with the maturing Digital Twin of an Organization (DTO) space, where vendors are expected to demonstrate proven capabilities.
Vendor ecosystem integration
The 2025 questions show that analyst groups are prioritizing solutions that exhibit ecosystem readiness—capable of integrating into hybrid environments and leveraging existing investments.
Rise of AI-driven insights
Questions related to data processing and normalization indicate that solutions enabling predictive analytics and AI-driven decision-making are becoming increasingly prominent. This aligns with industry demands for utilizing digital twins for strategic insights and real-time adaptability.
Section #3: Technology Excellence Criteria
Similar to the previous section, the topics of 2025 are pretty different from 2024. 
Evolving priorities and industry trends
Greater focus on advanced capabilities: The shift in emphasis from general business process modeling to advanced topics like "Real-Time Data Integration" and "AI-Driven Predictive Analytics" in 2025 highlights growing industry expectations for cutting-edge technologies.
Increased importance of integration: Questions about integration with IoT devices, ERP systems, and real-time data synchronization reflect the need for solutions that seamlessly connect with existing systems, showing a move toward interconnected ecosystems.
Strategic use of AI: The addition of AI-focused criteria emphasizes the industry’s reliance on AI for optimization, decision-making, and forecasting trends—key drivers for digital transformation.
Practical outcomes over theory: The focus on real-world applications, such as "high-fidelity models of operations" and "what-if analysis", indicates a shift from theoretical capabilities to practical, measurable impacts.
Implications for us
Highlight Advanced Features: Demonstrate AI, real-time data integration, and predictive capabilities in RFIs.
Emphasize Ecosystem Fit: Showcase interoperability with existing enterprise systems to align with analyst expectations.
Focus on Tangible Outcomes: Provide examples of real-world efficiency, cost savings, or operational improvements enabled by your solution.
