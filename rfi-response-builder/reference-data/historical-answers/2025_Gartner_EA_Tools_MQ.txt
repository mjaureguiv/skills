The architecture provides five ways of presentation and data access:

The persistence layer consists of multiple databases for best reliability and performance for different purposes

3. Postgres time series DB for efficient KPI storage
4. Proprietary reference catalog DB for access to vendor information

Q24: Describe how the integrity of data is ensured.

LeanIX employs the OAuth 2.0 protocol for technical user authentication across all our REST APIs.

Q25: What data backup and recovery options are available for the repository in your EA Tool Submission?
A: LX performs daily full backups of the database. All backups are encrypted via AES-256 bit cipher. Restoration testing is performed on a Quarterly basis. Backups are created in the respective region where the application is hosted via an automated job and replicated from Azure Managed Disk to Azure Blob Storage. Azure Blob Storage is configured with Read-Access Geo-Redundant Storage (RA-GRS). Configuration with RA-GRS ensures that the backups are replicated synchronously across three storage clusters in one datacenter in a single region and additionally asynchronously to another datacenter in a secondary region.

Additionally, customers can backup their own data via the workspace export button in the UI or programmatically via LX's open REST API endpoints. Some customers execute daily scripts to programmatically export and store their workspace data.

Q114: Describe your preferences of leveraging foundational AI models versus fine-tuned AI models in your EA Tool Submission to deliver augmented architecture development.
A: The main challenge leveraging GenAI in the context of EA is to make responses relevant for the customer and their specific situation. While fine-tuning would increase the general accuracy and improve the language of any LLM response, it would not improve relevancy for the customer. Backed by extensive research, we therefore prefer using foundational models and retrieval-augemented generation (RAG) over investing time and resources in (even customer-specific) fine-tuning of AI models, as it allows us to efficiently add customer-specific data to the LLM-context, leading to more relevant answers. With RAG, we also avoid the need to fine-tune multiple different LLMs or any new LLM versions again and again.
Fine tuning: High costs & complexity, low up-to-date rate, low transparency; good accuracy
RAG: Moderate costs & complexity, highly up-to-date, high transparency; a bit lower accuracy (needs prompt engineering), but much better relevancy

Q144: Fully describe your EA Tool SubmissionsÆ pricing model(s).
A: SAP LeanIX uses a transparent, scalable pricing model tailored to enterprise architecture use cases. It combines LeanIXÆs original logarithmic pricing with SAP commercial requirements in a graduated structure.

- Graduated Pricing Structure:  Pricing is based on sequential tiers of applications. Customers donÆt jump directly to the price for their total quantity, but gradually fill lower tiers. This aligns pricing with actual usage and growth.
- Per-Application Licensing: SAP LeanIX continues to license per the number of applications managed in SAP LeanIX, in increments of 100, with a 100-application minimum. This supports predictable scaling without significant upfront costs.
- Consistency across Products: The model applies across the SAP LeanIX suite, including APM, TRC, ARP, and capabilities like SaaS Discovery. Customers benefit from unified pricing logic rather than fragmented schemes.
- SAP Compliance: The model adheres to SAP procurement and pricing standards, enabling bundled offers and joint contracts for customers already using SAP solutions.

Customer Impact: This approach avoids steep price jumps when jumping from one tier to another. A global manufacturing customer chose SAP LeanIX over a leading competitor due partly to the transparent pricing. The customer expanded gradually without renegotiating contracts or incurring sudden cost increases.
Implementation Example: A customer managing 600 applications pays tiered rates by filling six 100-app increments. They avoid a flat fee of 600, ensuring costs scale with usage.

This pricing model supports cost-efficient adoption at every stage of the EA journey, making it easier for large enterprises to standardize, expand, and align EA practices across regions and business units.

Ģ Screen reader enablement: LX is technically prepared for screen reader support. This means that all the structural information and texts are handed over to assistive technology. Landmarks are used to provide a meaningful overview to screen readers.
