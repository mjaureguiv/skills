### ROW 16: d) Do you market and sell DTO platforms as a core capability?  
(If your primary business is  Enterprise Architecture (EA), Enterprise Business Process Analysis (EBPA), Enterprise Performance Management (EPM), Process Intelligence, Process Mining, Industrial Digital Twins and you are seldom used for E2E business operations or business operating models please indicate that in comments section)
**ANSWER:** Yes. SAP markets and sells Digital Twin of an Organization (DTO) capabilities as a core capability within its Business Transformation Management (BTM) solution area. Since 2021, SAP has acquired SAP Signavio and SAP LeanIX, combined with WalkMe, strengthening its DTO capabilities with each successive acquisition, and combining these solutions with other existing SAP portfolio capabilities.

SAP’s DTO is positioned as the foundation for establishing transformation as a continuous enterprise capability, not as isolated EA, BPM, or process mining tools. The solution delivers end-to-end enterprise observability and actionability across strategic planning, value management, business performance, operating models, processes, applications, data, integrations, user adoption, execution, and outcome measurement.

Key capabilities include strategy-to-value alignment, operating model design, process and performance intelligence, application and technology impact analysis, transformation roadmapping, dependency and scenario analysis, value realization tracking, and change adoption measurement. Together, these capabilities support all 12 DTO use cases defined by Gartner, spanning strategic, operational, and execution-focused scenarios.

SAP’s agentic layer, including Joule and SAP Build, offering a unified conversational experience, further operationalizes the DTO by enabling AI-assisted insight, orchestration, and execution across SAP Signavio, SAP LeanIX, WalkMe, and the broader SAP ecosystem.
**COMMENTS:** Our DTO supports composability on capability and landscape levels (process, EA, user behaviour), enhancing observability across strategic, business performance, application,  data, integrations, implementation and measurement layers. 
These enable organizations to build DTO's that are specific, enhance understanding of "as-is" and "to-be" states, and track value as it evolves across the enterprise.
