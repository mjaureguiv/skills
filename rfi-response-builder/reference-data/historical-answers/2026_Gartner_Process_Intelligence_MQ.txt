### Question 2: Please describe your product vision and roadmap for process intelligence in the next two to three years, including major focus areas.
**CHAR_LIMIT:** 2000
**ANSWER:** Process Intelligence is more than process mining — it is the foundation for driving intelligent, end‑to‑end and continuous process transformation.
Its evolution centers on three areas:
1. A single source of truth across vantage points (processes, tasks, networks), transformation dimensions (process, applications, data, people), and enterprise dimensions (risk, experience, strategy, etc.), enabling better visibility, data‑driven decisions, improved efficiency, and stronger cross‑team collaboration.
2. AI-driven operational intelligence: Process Intelligence will become an indispensable tool for everyday business users, playing a critical role in defining how companies design, operate, and govern AI Agents, helping to identify the most suitable workflows to automate, detect risks, enforce controls and guardrails, express relevant goals to drive effective behaviours, and ensure reliable, scalable execution.
3. A core enabler of the DTO. As a foundational capability for DTO initiatives, Process Intelligence provides a holistic view of operational dynamics, enabling continuous optimization and transformation.

Based on that, our roadmap reflects our commitment to these three areas:
1. Transform Process Intelligence into a knowledge layer for humans and AI: Use Process atoms as the unified currency of process knowledge, and Process networks as the connecting tissue; deepen integrations so this knowledge can be sourced from, shared with, and exposed to any business systems and knowledge layers.
2. Co-create value with AI: Deploy multi-agents as knowledge workers (with human oversight) to analyze process data, generate tasks and insights, perform unified value analysis, root‑cause via agents/atoms, and drive simulation and recommendations for transformation.
3. Expand the composite DTO: Extend process intelligence with Agent Mining, and integrate beyond the process view into enterprise architecture and digital adoption to strengthen human–machine interaction.

### Question 11: Please answer the following questions regarding your object centric process mining (OCPM) capabilities.
**ANSWER:** Use + or - signs on the left to Expand or collapse

  **SUB-Q:** Do you offer OCPM? If yes, please explain your offering in detail
  **ANSWER:** We view OCPM as key technique within our broader Process Networks vision. Our approach has three-pillars: 1) Out-of-the-box object-centric views for SAP data (Beta Dec 2025) - zero manual modeling. 2) A OCPM data modeling feature (GA Jan 2026) that allows users to define objects as real-life business documents and define case-centric perspectives 3) An OCPM analysis to discover processes without case-notions (GA in Signavio Labs in autumn 2025, foreseen in roadmap in general product in H1 2026).

  **SUB-Q:** What standards (if any) do you support for OCPM?
  **ANSWER:** Our platform natively supports the OCED (Object-Centric Event Data) conceptual framework. We have engineered our internal data schema to align with the principles established by the IEEE Task Force on Process Mining, specifically designed to handle the multi-dimensional nature of modern business processes (m:n relationships between events and objects).

  **SUB-Q:** How does OCPM integrate with process modeling and monitoring features?
  **ANSWER:** In our Object Centric model, customers can select objects that belong to a given process model, as well as depict how the objects flow in real life, with a no-code UI and no technical expertise required.
Once a leading object is selected, the respective analysis for process monitoring such as dashboards and respective widgets, threasholds and action, flows.

  **SUB-Q:** Provide a customer example where OCPM delivered unique insights.
  **ANSWER:** A client reported an issue tracking Sales Orders that are delayed by stock outs. To solve this, we are linking Sales Orders to their respective Purchase or Production Orders. By checking requirements at the material/plant level, we can identify exactly which orders will miss their delivery windows due to late supply. This would have been really complex in case-centric mining.

### Question 19: How do you offer task mining to the market, and if you do offer it, how do you distinguish yourself from competitors?
**GUIDANCE:** Answer Guidance: Please explain, including if these capabilities are through a partnership. Also explain how your process and task mining capabilities are connected.
**CHAR_LIMIT:** 2000
**ANSWER:** SAP Signavio differentiates itself firstly, through an Open Ecosystem Approach, moving away from the single-vendor task mining and task and process mining vendors. Second, we treat task level data as a critical telemetry layer for holistic transformation.

1. The Open Ecosystem
Unlike competitors with closed, proprietary recording agents, SAP Signavio leverages a dual-layered integration model:
- Deep SAP Ecosystem Integration: We utilize WalkMe (within the SAP suite) and SAP User Experience Management by KNOA for unparalleled visibility into user behavior within SAP and non-SAP environments (Salesforce, Oracle, etc.).
- Best-of-Breed Partnerships: We integrate validated partners like KYP.ai and Mimica AI, alongside UiPath. This allows customers to select the specific capture technology (e.g., AI-driven keystroke analysis vs. structured log capture) that fits their security and complexity needs.

2. Integration into Holistic Transformation
While competitors often provide standalone desktop analysis, SAP Signavio distinguishes itself by how it operationalizes data:
- Transformation over Analysis: Task mining data is embedded into the SAP Signavio Process Transformation Suite. This ensures task insights feed immediately into process redesign and transformation initiatives, rather than sitting in a siloed dashboard.
- End-to-End Orchestration: We link "micro" user actions to "macro" process KPIs. This allows organizations to see exactly how individual desktop inefficiencies impact enterprise-wide value realization and ERP migration timelines.
- Bridge to Automation: By using an open API and partner approach, we provide a seamless path from discovery to execution. Insights don't just identify bottlenecks; they generate the requirements for automation (RPA) or process standardized in the SAP S/4HANA core.

### Question 27: How do different personas interact and consume process models within your platform?
