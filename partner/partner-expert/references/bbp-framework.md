## Overview

A unified framework guiding strategic decisions on product innovation — when to build in-house, buy externally, or partner. This summary focuses on the frameworks, criteria, and processes. For the full deck, see link #1 in the registry.

## The Three Paths

### When to Build

Core to strategy, differentiating, and efficient to develop internally.

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Strategic Alignment** | Solution aligns with SAP's core priorities and long-term goals |
| **Core Competency** | Falls under SAP's domain expertise, or SAP can build expertise to stay ahead |
| **Competitive Differentiation** | Unique value via proprietary solution not achievable via buy/partner |
| **Cost Efficiency** | Internal development cost remains lower or comparable to alternatives |
| **IP Ownership** | Owning the IP is critical for competitive advantage or monetization |
| **Business Case** | Clear ROI and market demand |
| **Resource Support** | SAP has (or can fill) resource gaps to deliver effectively |
| **Time to Market** | Building won't unduly delay capturing market opportunities |
| **Opportunity Cost** | Won't delay more strategic roadmap items |
| **Portfolio Leverage** | Solution connects with or cross-sells other SAP products |

### When to Buy

Fastest path to fill critical capability or market gap.

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Time-to-Market** | Market opportunity is time-sensitive; acquisition delivers faster |
| **Proven Leadership** | Solution demonstrates clear market or technical leadership |
| **Capability Gap** | SAP lacks critical expertise to build in reasonable timeframe |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Acquisition de-risks high-stakes roadmap items |
| **Strategic Fit** | Addresses critical gap SAP should own and accelerate |
| **Business Case** | Justified ROI including cost, synergy, long-term alignment |
| **Integration Potential** | Can integrate into SAP's tech stack and product suite |
| **IP/Knowledge** | Gains critical IP or assets for sustained differentiation |
| **Talent & Culture** | Key talent can be retained; cultural fit exists |

### When to Partner

Extend reach through co-innovation and shared value.

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Market Access** | Partner provides access to industries/regions SAP can't penetrate alone |
| **Portfolio Enhancement** | Rounds out portfolio with complementary solutions |
| **Cost/Risk Sharing** | Reduces SAP's risk by sharing costs and responsibilities |
| **GTM Synergies** | Strong alignment on go-to-market execution |
| **Expertise Leverage** | Partner's domain expertise fills gaps in SAP's roadmap |
| **Speed to Market** | Ready solution meets needs without long lead times |
| **Global Scalability** | Partner can operate and execute globally |
| **Revenue Impact** | Clear revenue potential (direct and indirect) |
| **Commercial Model** | Clear model defined upfront (OEM, SolEx, EA, joint dev) |
| **Strategic Fit** | Aligns with SAP's long-term goals without creating unintended dependencies |
| **Governance** | Reviewed by stakeholders to avoid conflicts with existing/planned development |

## Partner Solution Progression (Volume → Value)

From lowest to highest SAP commitment:

1. **SAP Store Listed** — Digital self-service marketing, optional certification
2. **Spotlight+** — Active co-sell model, high-touch partner management, premium certification required
3. **Endorsed App** — SAP's preferred partner apps, co-marketing, premium certified. Invitation only.
4. **Solution Extension (SolEx)** — Re-sell on SAP paper, SAP-led GTM, premium qualification required. Invitation only.
5. **SAP Solution ("by SAP")** — SAP-led, not part of PE Build progression

## Strategic vs Volume Partnerships

| Characteristic | Strategic (Value) | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Big bets with category leaders covering core processes | Complementary, smaller portfolio additions |
| **Partners** | Market leaders with strong footprint (e.g., Icertis) | GSSPs, niche vendors, regional, startups |
| **Revenue** | >3M EUR annual partner revenue share to SAP | <1M EUR |
| **Portfolio fit** | Co-operative, may overlap SAP portfolio but unlocks critical segment | Limited scope extending SAP solution |
| **Commercial model** | Endorsed App target, SolEx for select cases | SAP Store target, uplevel possible |
| **GTM** | Strong direct GTM with dedicated sales | Partner-led GTM, limited niche market |
| **Whitespace** | Core functionality for customers | Optional differentiation |
| **Customer relevance** | Relevant for most SAP customers in that space | Subset of customers / niche |

## Partner Model Assessment Criteria

| Criteria | Co-Development | Inbound OEM | Outbound OEM | PE Build | Endorsed Apps | Solution Extensions |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **IP** | SAP-owned | Partner IP, SAP liability | Partner IP & liability | Partner IP & liability | Partner IP & liability | Partner IP, full SAP liability |
| **Market** | Significant | No standalone, critical req | Medium-significant | Niche/narrow | Medium-significant (1M ACV) | Significant (10M SW revenue) |
| **Portfolio** | Must-own, strategic | High strategic, completeness | Not required | Lower strategic | Cloud portfolio complement | Large-scale standalone app |
| **Tech** | Impacts SAP core | Packed into SAP solution | Embed/build-on SAP tech | Minimal SAP enablement | Moderate integration | Deep integration |

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **P&E Product Manager** | Owns product vision, whitespace strategy, roadmap. Drives Build/Buy/Partner decisions. |

## E2E Process (8 Phases)

### Phase 1: Strategic Planning & Alignment
- 3-Year Solution Area Strategy
- E2E Planning Sessions (yearly Q1/Q4)
- Quarterly Business Reviews
- Monthly Business Reviews
- Semester Plans

### Phase 2: Whitespace Identification & Classification
- Develop business capability matrix (outside-in & inside-out)
- Map existing SAP and ISV solutions
- Identify and prioritize whitespaces
- Classify each as Build, Buy, or Partner
- Partner whitespaces become "Partner Spaces"

### Phase 3: Determine Partner Approach
- Evaluate model options: SolEx, Endorsed, OEM, Co-Dev, Spotlight+, Store
- Consider market opportunity, strategic importance, GTM complexity
- Classify as strategic/value vs. volume play

### Phase 4: Partner Analysis & Discovery
- Long-list based on market insight (IDC, SAP Store, analysts)
- Evaluate partner size, product fit, growth ambition
- Shortlist preferred candidates
- Conduct discovery calls to validate strategic fit

### Phase 5: Business Case & Nominations
- Partner engagement and due diligence
- Market & revenue assessment (2yr historical + 3yr projection)
- Joint value proposition & GTM plan
- Submit to 3iaB → PPWG for governance approval

### Phase 6: Onboarding & Enablement
- Contracts and commercial agreements
- Material codes, cloud delivery setup
- Premium Certification (Endorsed) or Premium Qualification (SolEx)
- Non-technical enablement (business planning, marketing, demand gen)
- Technical enablement (integrations, demos, environment setup)

### Phase 7: GTM Execution
- Integrate partner solutions into LoB sales plays
- Joint events (PartnerConnect, Sapphire, industry events)
- Demand generation campaigns
- Field engagement and co-selling motions
- Pipeline tracking

### Phase 8: Performance Management
- Annual budget per partner solution
- Lead-to-opportunity-to-deal conversion tracking
- Partner QBRs and LoB Regional ISV QBRs
- Premium Certification renewal
- Strategic relevance & lifecycle assessment (progression/regression decisions)

## Governance Cadences

| Cadence | Frequency | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|

| Quarterly Business Review | Quarterly | 60 min | Performance retrospective & traction |
| Monthly Business Review | Monthly (non-QBR months) | 90 min | Health check across functions |
| Partner & Portfolio QBR/MBR | Min quarterly | 30-60 min | Strategic partner performance |
| PPWG | Monthly | 60 min | Nomination approvals (Endorsed/SolEx) |

## RACI Summary

Key accountability pattern:
- **R/A (Responsible/Accountable)**: P&E Product Manager — for whitespace ID, classification, partner approach, business case
- **R/A**: ISV Portfolio Lead — for partner intake, operational enablement, PPWG, partner management
- **C (Consulted)**: Product Marketing, Product Development, Sales/GTM, Product Success
- **I (Informed)**: SIX, Legal, Corporate Development
