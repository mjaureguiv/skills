# Inspiring Storyteller Skill

## Purpose

Transform data, slides, and dry content into compelling human speeches that captivate leadership audiences. Make presentations memorable, exciting, and impactful.

---

## When to Use

- Leadership presentations and kick-offs
- Initiative announcements
- Quarterly reviews (QBRs, PPRs)
- Pitching new ideas to stakeholders
- Any time you have 2-3 minutes to make an impression

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Write Like You Speak
- NO bullet points pasted together
- Flowing, connected sentences
- Natural transitions between ideas
- Conversational tone — like talking to the room

### 2. Positive Framing
- Frame challenges as **discoveries**, not problems
- Frame gaps as **opportunities**, not failures
- Never finger-point or blame teams
- Focus on what we're building, not what's broken

### 3. Simple and Clean
- One idea per paragraph
- Short sentences that punch
- Remove jargon — anyone should understand
- If it sounds like a bot wrote it, rewrite it

### 4. Create Intrigue
- Start with a hook: "What if I told you..."
- Build tension: "We thought... but then we discovered..."
- Deliver the insight: "The real story is..."
- End with action: "Join us on..."

---

## The Story Arc

Every great speech follows this structure:

```
1. THE HOOK
   → Grab attention with a question or surprising statement

2. THE SETUP
   → Share what everyone assumed or believed

3. THE DISCOVERY
   → Reveal the insight that changes everything

4. THE OPPORTUNITY
   → Reframe the situation positively

5. THE ACTION
   → What we're doing about it (workstreams, initiatives)

6. THE INVITATION
   → Call to action — bring them along
```

## Example Transformation

### Before (Slide Bullets):
```
• 481,000 licenses sold
• 81,000 activated (17%)
• Actual usage: 342,000
• Gap in licensing governance
• 4 workstreams planned
• Kick-off March 19
```

### After (Human Speech):
```
What if I told you we've been underestimating ourselves?

In Signavio Process Governance, we sold 481,000 licenses.
Our reports showed 81,000 activated. That number made us
curious. We thought — there must be more to this story.

So we looked closer. And we discovered something exciting.

Actual usage? 342,000. Our customers aren't just buying —
they're using. They're succeeding. SPG is four times more
successful than we realized.

And here's the thing — if this gap exists in SPG, it likely
exists across MxG. Same licensing challenges. Same governance
gaps. Same hidden potential waiting to be unlocked.

That's why this initiative isn't just for SPG. We're going
MxG-wide...
```

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- ✅ Start with a question or bold statement
- ✅ Use "we" language — inclusive
- ✅ Let numbers land — pause after big stats
- ✅ End with a clear invitation
- ✅ Keep it under 2-3 minutes
- ✅ Make it positive and forward-looking

### DON'T:
- ❌ Read bullet points
- ❌ Blame other teams or systems
- ❌ Use corporate jargon
- ❌ List everything — pick the highlights
- ❌ End without a call to action
- ❌ Sound like a bot or a press release

---

## Speech Length Guide

| Time | Word Count | Use Case |
|------|------------|----------|
| 90 seconds | ~200 words | Quick pitch, teaser |
| 2 minutes | ~300 words | Standard update |
| 3 minutes | ~450 words | Full initiative intro |

---

## Process

1. **Input**: User provides slides, data, or topic
2. **Extract**: Identify the key insight and numbers
3. **Frame**: Find the positive angle / discovery
4. **Structure**: Apply the story arc
5. **Write**: Create flowing human speech
6. **Refine**: Remove jargon, shorten, add punch
7. **Deliver**: Format for easy reading/presenting

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Confident** — you believe in what you're sharing
- **Curious** — frame discoveries as exciting
- **Inclusive** — "we" not "I" or "they"
- **Human** — like talking to smart colleagues
- **Energizing** — leave them wanting to act
