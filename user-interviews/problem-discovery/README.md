# Problem Discovery Interviews

Exploratory user interviews to uncover unknown problems and opportunities.

## When to Use

- Starting a new product or feature initiative
- Exploring an unknown opportunity space
- Leadership gives a vague directive ("build an AI agent for X")
- You need to understand the current state before defining solutions

## What This Does

Helps you:
- Generate interview guides for exploratory research
- Review your draft questions for bias and assumptions
- Practice discovery interviewing techniques

## How to Use

**Generate a guide:**
```
/interview-discovery

I'm exploring opportunities for [area]. Target users are [profile].
Help me create an interview guide.
```

**Review your questions:**
```
/interview-discovery

Review my discovery questions:
1. [Your questions...]
```

**Practice interviewing:**
```
/interview-discovery

Let's practice a discovery interview about [topic]. Play the role of a user.
```

## Key Principles

### DO
- Ask open-ended questions
- Let users lead the conversation
- Follow interesting threads
- Stay curious about everything

### DON'T
- Assume what the problem is
- Mention solutions you're considering
- Ask about "pain points" directly
- Lead with closed questions

## Sample Questions

| Good | Why |
|------|-----|
| "Walk me through a typical day" | Open, lets them prioritize |
| "Can you show me how you handle that?" | Observational |
| "What should feel easier?" | Softer than "pain points" |
| "What workarounds have you developed?" | Reveals hidden friction |

## Expected Output

After 5-8 discovery interviews, you should have:
- 7-10 potential opportunity areas
- Understanding of real workflows (vs. stated)
- Themes and patterns across users
- Surprises and unexpected insights

Take these to [Problem Validation](../problem-validation/) next.
